## Powers

### Pheromone — Precognitive Disruption
The defining engineered ability. A biochemical output — secreted passively or triggered consciously — that disrupts Riley Thomas's one-second precognitive flash specifically. His premonitions return nothing in her presence. Not a hole in the future; a hole in his ability to read her as a threat.

Implication: A.E.G.I.S. studied Riley long enough and closely enough to map how his precognitive ability functions biologically and synthesize a targeted counter. This is not passive observation. Someone in ACAD or SMAB ran experiments on him as a subject, not an asset. The file being withheld from Riley is evidence of that program.

May have secondary effects on others — mild disorientation, impaired threat-reading — but the Riley counter is the designed purpose.

### Kinetic Redirection
Her body has been trained and structurally modified to never absorb force straight on. Everything glances. She finds the path of least resistance instinctively — weight shifts, contact angles, redirection points — and turns applied force back into her own angle of attack.

This is readable as extreme martial conditioning plus structural modification rather than a discrete superpower. She doesn't block. She redirects. The more aggressively an opponent engages, the worse their position becomes.

In practice: a web fired at her doesn't bounce off — she moves into it at the exact angle that makes it wrap wrong, and she's already using that tension to pull the shooter off balance. Force applied to her becomes her tool.

**Tell before demonstration:** She catches things she shouldn't. Hits don't land right. Her stance is always slightly angled, always already adjusting — she's never fully static.

### Structural Modification — Joint Reinforcement
Her joints don't give at the points a trained fighter targets. Locks don't work. Throws don't land clean. She's not stronger than a peak-conditioned opponent — she's structured differently. Techniques that end most fights don't end this one.

### Restructured Pain Response
Pain doesn't interrupt her motor function. She registers it, files it, keeps moving. The program didn't remove the signal — it severed the signal from the response. She absorbs damage and continues in a way that is deeply unsettling. Her threat isn't that she's hard to hurt. It's that hurting her doesn't work the way it should.

The scarring on her body tells you she has survived things she shouldn't have. She was built to complete the mission even if she doesn't walk away.

## Design Notes

Scythe is a precision instrument, not a superweapon. Her power set is narrow and specific — she was built for a particular kind of target. Against anyone else she is dangerous but not exceptional. Against Riley Thomas, she is the one fight his precognition cannot account for.

She knows she was built to counter Riley. That knowledge is part of her operational identity. What she does with it — whether she is compliant, conflicted, or something else — is unresolved.

Her current mission is surveillance, not neutralization. A.E.G.I.S. is watching Riley through her. The combat capability exists as contingency, not mandate — she can handle an engagement if Riley gets close enough to make her, but the job is to stay invisible. The fact that she has a full counter-combat kit for a surveillance operative says something about what A.E.G.I.S. expects might eventually happen.

## A.E.G.I.S. Program Implications

- Likely trained from childhood; A.E.G.I.S.'s ethical boundaries around enhanced subjects have only become more extreme over time, not less
- Her existence implies a classified program that treated Riley as a subject to be studied and countered — not just an asset to be managed
- The deliberate withholding of AEGIS-SYC-REDACTED from Riley is an institutional choice, not bureaucratic oversight
- The surveillance mission raises an open question: what is A.E.G.I.S. waiting to see? What would change the mission from watch to act?
- What else has A.E.G.I.S. built that the Alliance doesn't know about?
