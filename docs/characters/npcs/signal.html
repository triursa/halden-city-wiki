Signal

"Absolute power corrupts absolutely. The revolution has begun."

At a Glance

Field Value
Real Name Unknown
Status At large
Type Cult-of-personality antagonist, master social engineer
Powers None currently — formerly a brief Renegade Force host (voluntarily relinquished)
Threat Level High — ideological, systemic, long-game

Who Signal Is

Signal calls himself a prophet of the coming age. Impossibly patient and surgically precise, he orchestrates public crises not for chaos — but for revelation.

A master hacker and social engineer, he hijacks networks, smart infrastructure, and private data streams with unnerving ease. His broadcasts are clean, cinematic, and disturbingly calm. He places people in impossible moral dilemmas and streams the results live, inviting the public to watch, vote, comment, and condemn.

He does not claim to create monsters. He claims to reveal them.

Signal believes society is already rotten — heroes included. According to him, all it takes is pressure, anonymity, and an audience. Underneath the skin, underneath the masks, humanity will always choose itself.

He wants followers. Clicks. Devotion. He speaks in sermons, not threats. His symbol appears like a watermark across every feed he hijacks. His voice is measured, almost compassionate.

The Renegade Force History

Signal was briefly a Renegade Force host, operating underground. He voluntarily relinquished the Force as part of his ideological plan — he is building a movement against super-dependence, and holding a cosmic power while doing so would undermine his own message. His "ordinary human" status in public records is a half-truth. He is ordinary now. He was not always.

He retains no residual Force abilities.

Current Operations

Signal is building toward an exposure event. He already holds a recording of Riley Thomas threatening Carter Williams in his hospital room. He engineered the hospital situation specifically to obtain this — he predicted Recluse would show up, and he was right.

He is two steps ahead. He is waiting for the right moment. When he finally acts, he intends for the whole world to be watching.

Voice & Characterization Notes

  • Focused and cold, not preachy
  • Shorter and colder is better — places a question and exits rather than building to a conclusion
  • Speaks in sermons but keeps them brief; the weight is in what he doesn't say
  • Devotion and cult-of-personality dynamics — he cultivates followers, not fear
  • Never sounds angry. That's what makes him dangerous.
