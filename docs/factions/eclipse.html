Eclipse

The shadow in the infrastructure.

At a Glance

Field Value
Type Antagonist organization — scope and structure undetermined
Status Active — presence confirmed, full picture unknown
Public profile None. Not a known entity to the general public.
Known to A.E.G.I.S. (flagged, not confirmed); select Alliance members

What Is Known

Eclipse is not a street gang and not a conventional supervillain operation. The name appears in A.E.G.I.S. files in connection with anomalous events that don't fit cleaner categorical explanations. It is less a confirmed organization than a pattern that keeps showing up when something goes wrong and the causal chain doesn't fully close.

Confirmed associations:

  • The 2020 Glass District gravity core collapse — possible Eclipse connection flagged, unconfirmed. The nature of the connection (did Eclipse cause it? facilitate it? benefit from it?) is not documented.
  • Vane Capital Group — Casimir Vane's unusually fast growth is one of multiple threads that may connect to Eclipse backing. Not confirmed.

Working assumption: Eclipse operates by embedding itself into existing legitimate structures rather than building its own. It does not announce itself. It positions assets, advantages, and contingencies, and waits.

Confirmed Operations

Biotech Research Arm

Eclipse funded a biotech research program focused on battlefield survivability and cellular reinforcement under extreme stress. The researcher was Dr. Ivan Romneya, a morally flexible scientist who appears to have been an unwilling participant in the program's full scope. When Ivan used an unstable experimental compound to save a dying child (Rhys) without authorization, Eclipse sent retrieval operatives. Ivan was tortured for access codes and identifiers and did not survive. The child — now known as Noc — escaped with the compound still running through their system.

Eclipse classifies Rhys as an unretrieved asset. The falsified death record Ivan created may still be holding, or may have been identified. Either way, the compound in Rhys's body is proprietary research and Eclipse has never formally closed the file.

Confirmed connection to a current PC. Handle with care.

Structure

Unknown. It is not clear whether Eclipse has a central leadership structure, a distributed cell model, or something else entirely. What is clear is that it has resources, patience, and either luck or foreknowledge.
