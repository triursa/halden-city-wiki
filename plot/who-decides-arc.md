# "Who Decides" — Arc 1 Beat Sheet

> Signal arc. Lean planning reference — leave room to improvise.
> Approximate pacing: 2 beats per session.

---

## The Through-Line

Signal forces the city — and the players — to answer one question: *Who gave these people the right to decide?*

His diagnosis is not entirely wrong. That's what makes him dangerous.

---

## Beats

---

### Beat 1 — The Handoff

The Alliance assembles the new team at Alliance Tower for a formal reveal. The old guard is present — but not for long. Mid-ceremony, the Alliance is called away to respond to a threat abroad. (This is the first signal of Darkstar's involvement — the "threat" will later be revealed as the Renegade Force attacking Darkstar's ship.) The new team is left alone in the tower, partially introduced to the city, fully on their own.

**GM Notes:**
- Use this beat to establish team dynamics before pressure hits
- The Alliance's departure should feel matter-of-fact, not dramatic — this is how it works, now it's your problem
- Plant the detail that the threat is off-world; don't elaborate yet

---

### Beat 2 — The Press Room

The new team steps into a press conference to formally introduce themselves to the city. A Senator is present to publicly endorse the Alliance's new initiative.

Mid-conference, ersatz A.E.G.I.S. agents enter the room. They attack and extract the Senator. Riley pursues the kidnappers. The players deal with the agents.

**The reveal underneath the masks:** The agents are PROMETHEUS constructs — cyborg cadavers in A.E.G.I.S. uniforms, not humans. The bodies are wrong on close inspection. Something Eclipse-adjacent.

**GM Notes:**
- The fight should feel like a public embarrassment as much as a threat — cameras are rolling
- Riley's pursuit keeps him off the board; the players have to close this themselves
- Leave the Eclipse thread as a thread, not an answer — a logo, a serial number, something they have to pull on

---

### Beat 3 — Signal's First Broadcast

Before the team can regroup, Signal hijacks every feed.

He broadcasts the press room fight back to the public — cut to make the new team look reactive and outmatched. Then he cuts to the Alliance's location: the Renegade Force host (Carter Williams) is destroying Darkstar's ship. The old guard, Earth's most powerful heroes, are struggling.

Signal's message: *These are your protectors. Watch carefully.*

He announces his initiative: one secret revealed per day until humanity decides for itself whether it wants to be governed by people with powers it didn't choose and can't control.

**GM Notes:**
- Darkstar should have an established public profile as a known threat the Alliance has never fully neutralized — the optics of him being attacked by an unknown host land harder if the city already fears him
- Signal's tone: calm, almost sympathetic. He is not angry. He is concerned.
- End on his sign-off — a question, not a threat. He places it and leaves.

---

### Beat 4 — Secret Drop 1 & 2 (One Session)

**Reveal 1 — Starfall:**
Signal surfaces evidence that Kiara Je-rach was engineered as a weapon and sent to Earth to determine whether it should be destroyed. She defected. But Signal's framing: *The Alliance's cosmic ally was built to kill you. She changed her mind. You're welcome to trust that.*

**Reveal 2 — Sterling / Darkstar:**
Signal reveals that Cosmic Knight is the biological son of Darkstar — the alien threat currently in the news. Engineered in his lab. Designed for a purpose. *The new team's heavy hitter was built by the enemy. Ask him what he was built to do.*

**GM Notes:**
- Reveal 1 damages the Alliance's credibility abstractly; Reveal 2 hits Sterling personally and publicly
- Jason's reaction to Sterling matters here — does he trust him, or does Nightfall's kill of Artemis make him suspicious of anything alien-adjacent?
- Sterling doesn't have to respond to the team immediately; let it sit

---

### Beat 5 — Nightfall Comes In

Morgan Vale approaches the team — not attacking, not threatening. Asking.

They believe heroes ultimately serve humanity. They want to be part of something that does. What they did to Artemis was real, and they are not pretending otherwise. But they are here.

Jason has to decide what to do with that.

**GM Notes:**
- Do not resolve this in one scene. Nightfall's presence on or near the team should be an ongoing friction point for Jason throughout the rest of the arc.
- Nightfall's reformation is sincere — but sincerity doesn't erase what happened. Let both things be true.
- This is Jason's beat, not the team's. The team's response to Nightfall is secondary to what it costs Jason.
- Nightfall's knowledge of Eclipse/PROMETHEUS infrastructure is a potential in-world reason the team eventually accepts (or tolerates) them — they have information. That's a GM lever, not a requirement.

---

### Beat 6 — PROMETHEUS

The Eclipse thread from the press room leads somewhere. Whether through Arachne's network, a captured construct, or active investigation — the team finds a PROMETHEUS facility.

Inside: the construction infrastructure for the cyborg cadavers. Blank-slate supersoldiers built from corpses and Eclipse biotech. A terminal. An email chain. Casimir Vane's name on an order demanding the program accelerate.

**GM Notes:**
- The facility should feel wrong before it feels dangerous — something about the scale of it
- Don't hand them the Vane/Signal connection here. Vane is Eclipse money. Signal is a separate thread. That link comes at the Gala.
- Let them take something out of here — evidence, a construct, a name — that they bring to the Gala

---

### Beat 7 — Secret Drop 3 & 4 (One Session)

**Reveal 3 — Paragon Force:**
Signal publishes evidence that the Paragon Force can be consciously released and directed by its host — and that the previous Paragon *chose* Ethan Roberts. The Force was not random. It was handed off. Signal's framing: *Your protector was selected by his predecessor. He has always had the option to put it down. He has not. Ask him why.*

**Reveal 4 — Half-Life / Decay:**
Signal outs Casper's history as Decay. The villain record. The people hurt. Whatever A.E.G.I.S. has on file. *One of your new heroes has a file. Read it.*

**GM Notes:**
- Reveal 3 is the setup for the climax — plant it here so it lands when Signal uses it at the end
- Reveal 4 is Casper's personal crisis and a team trust moment — does the team already know? Do they respond with doubt or solidarity?
- These two in the same session creates a compressed pressure — the team is managing Ethan's public credibility problem while also absorbing a revelation about one of their own

---

### Beat 8 — The Gala

Vane Capital's annual fundraising gala. The team has reason to be there — either through invitation, cover identities, or the PROMETHEUS evidence pointing here.

Casimir Vane is present. Charming, generous, apparently genuine. The team can observe, investigate, and work the room.

**The find:** Something at the Gala — a conversation, a file, a face they recognize — confirms that Vane's Eclipse funding connects upstream to Signal's operation. Vane is not Signal. But Signal's campaign would not be possible without Vane's resources.

**What this triggers:** The players now know. Signal knows they know. The endgame begins.

**GM Notes:**
- The Gala should have genuine social texture — Casimir is not obviously a villain in person. Make the players work for the connection.
- Whatever they find should feel earned, not handed to them
- End the session on the moment of discovery, not the confrontation — let the weight of it land before they act

---

### Beat 9 — Signal's Endgame

Signal moves on all fronts simultaneously.

**The tape:** Signal releases the Riley/Carter hospital recording — Riley threatening Carter Williams, alone, off-books, on Avi's behalf. Recluse's moral authority collapses publicly.

**The Riverside crisis:** A coordinated Eclipse/PROMETHEUS attack on Riverside Ward. Civilian casualties. Maximum media exposure. The players are forced to respond — visibly, messily, in front of cameras that Signal controls the feed for.

**The trap:** Signal broadcasts Paragon's location and corners him publicly, forcing the choice the Reveal 3 setup built toward: release the Force and prove Signal's point that one person shouldn't hold it — or hold it and prove Signal's other point that power never voluntarily surrenders itself.

**Ethan releases the Force.** Signal wins the argument, even if the players stop the attack.

**Riley's collapse:** Somewhere in the chaos, Riley pushes the deteriorating body too far. He does not go down because Signal put him there. He goes down because he was already going down. Signal just provided the occasion.

**Signal escapes.** The arc does not resolve him.

**GM Notes:**
- The partial win is the honest shape of this arc. Don't frame it as failure.
- The players should have meaningfully affected things — the Riverside casualties are lower because they were there, the PROMETHEUS network is damaged, the Gala evidence exists
- What they could not stop: Signal's argument landing, Paragon giving up the Force, Riley's collapse
- Worst-case state going into Arc 2: supers officially outlawed, the team operating as vigilantes, the Alliance fractured

---

## Arc End State

- Paragon Force released — Ethan Roberts, ordinary human
- Riley Thomas in a coma — self-inflicted via deterioration, not Signal's hand
- Alliance fractured and delegitimized
- Signal at large — real name and Renegade history still unknown
- PROMETHEUS network damaged but not destroyed
- Vane/Eclipse connection documented but not yet publicly proven
- Nightfall's status on or adjacent to the team: unresolved
